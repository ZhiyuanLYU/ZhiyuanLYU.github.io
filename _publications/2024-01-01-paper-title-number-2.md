Density-equalizing maps are a class of mapping methods in which the shape deformation is driven by prescribed density information. While such maps have been used for planar domains and open surface parameterization, the case of closed surfaces is less explored. This paper introduces a method for computing spherical density-equalizing maps for genus-0 closed surfaces. The approach first computes a conformal parameterization of the genus-0 closed surface onto the unit sphere, and then performs density equalization on that spherical domain. Bijectivity is guaranteed using quasi-conformal theory. The method also incorporates harmonic energy and landmark constraints to achieve landmark-aligned mappings that balance different distortion measures. Applications demonstrated include surface registration, remeshing, and data visualization.
