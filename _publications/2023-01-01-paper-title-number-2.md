This paper proposes a two-stage algorithm for computing spherical parameterizations that combine quasiconformal mapping and optimal mass transportation (OMT). The method aims to achieve both low geometric distortion and area-preserving properties for genus-0 closed surfaces. In the first stage, a quasiconformal map is computed to minimize angular distortion. In the second stage, the OMT framework is applied to further optimize the area preservation while maintaining the conformal properties. The combined approach provides a flexible framework for balancing different distortion measures in spherical parameterization. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method for various applications in computer graphics and geometry processing.
