Density-equalizing maps are a class of mapping methods in which the shape deformation is driven by prescribed density information. However, the bijectivity and local geometric distortions of the mappings are often uncontrolled, and prior work mainly addresses simply-connected surfaces rather than those with more complex topologies. This paper presents a novel approach for computing bijective density-equalizing quasiconformal (DEQ) flattening maps for multiply-connected open surfaces. The method formulates the density diffusion process as a quasiconformal flow, enabling control over local geometric distortion and guaranteeing bijectivity through an energy minimization problem involving the Beltrami coefficient. An iterative scheme is developed that optimizes both the shape of the target planar circular domain and the density-equalizing quasiconformal map onto it. The method also supports landmark constraints for consistent feature alignment and is applicable to simply-connected open surfaces as well. Validation on synthetic and real examples demonstrates effectiveness in computer graphics and medical imaging applications.
