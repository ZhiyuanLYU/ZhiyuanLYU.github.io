Density-equalizing maps are a class of mapping methods in which the shape deformation is driven by prescribed density information. While existing methods mainly focus on spherical targets for genus-0 closed surfaces, this paper introduces a novel approach for computing density-equalizing maps onto ellipsoidal targets. The proposed method first computes a conformal parameterization of the genus-0 closed surface onto an ellipsoid, and then performs density equalization on the ellipsoidal domain. The bijectivity of the mapping is guaranteed using quasiconformal theory. The ellipsoidal target provides more flexibility for surfaces that are elongated or anisropic, enabling better density equalization results. Applications in surface registration, remeshing, and data visualization are demonstrated.
